Island Archives

Island Archives is a program to provide online access to the recorded history of Prince Edward Island. Housed at the Robertson Library at the University in Prince Edward Island, the knowledge here was gathered in partnership with several communities and organizations, thanks to generous financial support from a number of individuals and organizations. Launched in 2010, Island Archives uses Islandora, a community-run open-source software project that was created at the University of Prince Edward Island.

PEI Exhibit Collections

  • Island Lives

    Books about Prince Edward Island, highlighting community, church, and local histories. Made possible by a private donation.
  • Island Voices Oral histories (spoken audio), featuring the audio archive from Reg "Dutch" Thompson's "The Bygone Days." Supported by the Inukshuk Foundation.
  • Island Newspapers Historic newspapers of Prince Edward Island, including The Guardian from the 1890s to the 1950s.
  • Bowing Down Home Fiddle music traditions on Prince Edward Island, curated by Ken Perlman and in partnership with of the Canadian Museum of History.
  • Island Imagined Historical maps, atlases, and gazetteers of Prince Edward Island, produced in collaboration with Public Archives and Records Office and the PEI Museum and Heritage Foundation.
  • PEI Legislative Documents Full text of the Journals of the Legislative Assembly of Prince Edward Island starting from the 32nd General Assembly of Prince Edward Island in March of 1894 to the present day. In partnership with the PEI Legislature, the Public Archives and Records Office, and the Government Services Library.
  • The Island Magazine Full text of The Island Magazine from 1976 to 1996, published by and provided in partnership with the PEI Museum and Heritage Foundation.
  • The Prince Edward Island Magazine The Prince Edward Island Magazine was published by Archibald Irwin from 1899-1905.
